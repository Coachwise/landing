Most athletes keep their training in three places: an app for the barbell, a notebook for the wall, and a spreadsheet the coach opens once a week. None of it adds up to anything.

That's the problem Coachwise was built for: **one app for strength training and climbing, with the coach writing the plan inside it.** It's now in beta.

## What works today

- **Logging a session.** Sets, reps, weight and RPE for the barbell; grade, send type and hangboard for the wall. A half-finished session stays on your phone and picks up where you left it.
- **Your coach's plan.** The coach writes the week and it lands on your phone. Today's session opens ready to go, and the moment you finish it, your coach sees it.
- **Coach packages.** A coach builds a package and sells it; what an athlete pays lands in the coach's wallet, and they cash out when they want to.
- **Assessments and records.** Take a test, log the record, and show the ones you choose on your profile, in the order you choose.
- **Passwordless sign-in.** A phone number, a one-time code, done.

The app is **Persian and right-to-left from the ground up** — not a translation bolted onto an English app.

## What doesn't yet

- **The stores.** Coachwise isn't on Google Play or the App Store yet. Until it is, the download buttons on this site stay disabled — they go live the moment the listings do.
- **Beta means beta.** Things will break. If something doesn't work, say so; at this stage feedback is worth more than anything else.

## What's next

The short-term goal is unglamorous: ship the store listings, and polish the paths people actually walk every day — logging a session, following a plan, and a coach working with their athletes.

If you're a coach and you want your athletes on Coachwise now, this beta is exactly what it's for.
